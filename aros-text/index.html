E 10° 12’ 21.127”

to live is to be open

si tu me permets, I hesitate

je dois t’avouer que,

Bolaño, Tres) / I stumble and hesitate / as an eroticism (cf. Lorde) is revealed in the interplay of our languages / one I can no longer keep to myself (Belgicisme)



I pause for words,

words I harboured,

kept and left unspoken

vraiment envoûtantall evening, I have been mesmerised by her radiance / my French revealing imperfections I want to hide / in this niterie, my hands unsure where they need to be, our legs brush, and I dare not hold her gaze



for 204,138,435 seconds, or

nonante-cinq millions

E 10° 12’ 0.716”

to live is to risk all

let me put your body to sleep,

Aros III — All I Need to Hear

N 56° 8’ 54.751”

E 10° 12’ 13.168”



at 8.25 am I text Joe

later he will say that

to live is to endure

love’s cruel timing



I got back to the hotel

alone; she returned later on,

‘goodnight’ waiting elsewhere,

mine one of quiet longing,

packing bags, to carry on



a deep ache to hold you

just a little bit longer,

for once to return home

with a small palimpsest

of one’s delicate affection

égratignéa wound deeper than a graze, with incredible precision / cut to the rhythm of a cibell / carried without demanding anything of her / after Lully’s Atys (Brussels, 19 November 1700)—si belle, I did not want to be spared it in my skin



somewhere two floors below

she smiles, as I put a record

on, pour mon soulagement ;my dancing will not stop because you are not here / I am dancing because we were there / if it wasn’t for the passing inyeon my Layla I’d never have gone home

I would have regretted it

for longer than the days

I withheld these words,

fragilely kept in amber—



now 263,142 seconds, or

4385 minutes, or
