Eros I — About You

N 56° 9’ 23.283”

E 10° 12’ 21.127”

 

at 2.23 am I text Joe

si tu me permets, I hesitate

je dois t’avouer que,

 

I lied; I only hesitated in

my pronunciation, as I wrote

this down less than an hour

after meeting you I don’t

know why—

 

I didn’t pause for words,

I constructed them

meticulously, because

I wanted to get it

really fucking right

after 6 years,

5 months and 19 days,

almost two-thirds

of a decade, of

thinking no one

would spark my love,

I wanted to get this

completely right—

 

tu dégages quelque chose

de tellement rayonnant,

et ton sourire est si

vraiment envoûtant

 

I almost wanted to vomit

so wrecked by nerves

for saying those words

hoping you did not notice

how I positioned my leg

to be touched by yours

 

I meant it; it wasn’t

a literary game, or

an erotic game, like

these poems; and

suddenly, now, after

 

204,138,435 seconds, or

3,402,307 minutes, or

56,705 hours, or

2362 days, or

however long ago

I had stopped counting

any proper heartbeats—

 

finally, I felt that maybe

–so excitedly I could

barely breathe or speak–

things might get better

if we are together

E 10° 12’ 0.716”

 

at 6.55 pm I text Joe

and feel it fully

 

in the middle of the night

during a panic attack

I draft a screaming email

to my therapist; she will

understand the gravity of

this devastation, surely

 

I console myself by remembering

I don’t; I resent that this

poem is now about me

love is about giving

some identity away

I lost 3 kg in 3 days

 

I poured images upon images

hiding behind references

within references, obscured

in multiple languages;

knowing that no one will

decode all of them; it’s safe

 

in seven languages

the poem guarded against

what I am too afraid to admit:

 

I am in love with you

 

I remember nothing

but you Mylène

too elated to move

to eat to sleep

 

I have not cared about

kissing sex

such pleasures lost

after exes’s consentless

despoliations my body

since I was fourteen

and then again at 25

 

now, at 32, in a uni hall,

you. I

encounter a soul

close to mine

through a language

of bodies coup d'œil

 

“are you okay?”; you asked

kindly, after I fell silent

for a few seconds too long

because your eyes, with

their ridiculously piercing

vintage Prussian blue hue

 

Yes; I am doing it again, but

how else do you expect me to

write the unwritable?

 

invaded my being

leaving me speechless

 

I kept getting lost,

sunk

in thought, distracted

if you would mistake it,

I don’t

 

 

really need you,
